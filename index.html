We're offering online courses that teach what medical and law students learn in their respective programs.

No, you won't get a degree out of it.

You're learning this for the joy of learning. It just so happens that this knowledge is incredibly useful, and quite possibly more practical than 99% of what you learned in college.

Your donation will go into the hiring of medical/law professors and students as we translate the material we have (from Harvard, Stanford, and Yale) for online consumption. This is not a trivial task.

Medical education is particularly difficult because of the underlying science foundation (biology, chemistry, math, physics) the student is expected to know before starting med school.

We won't be teaching the basic sciences (such material is plentiful online), but we'll link to resources as necessary.

FAQ

So the course is a series of lecture videos?
It's much more than that. It's an asynchronous learning environment consisting of online videos, class notes, a discussion forum, and testing components.

Many aspects of this type of highly specialized education cannot be learned by pointing a camera at an instructor standing in front of a blackboard; for example, medical students need to actually attempt procedures in order to learn them.

While it's impossible to give our students such practice, we'll instead record multiple students attempting these procedures for the first time under guidance of an instructor to simulate the experience.

Similarly, in law school, the classroom environment and method of inquiry is an important aspect of learning. Simply having a single camera on the professor is insufficient in conveying the experience of the Socratic method, so our videos will include both students and teacher.
Will I get anything after completing the course?
Upon completing the course with a minimum score, you'll receive a certificate of completion. But it's mostly useless, as we're not accredited in any way, so having the certificate in no way gives you the ability to actually call yourself a doctor or a lawyer. In fact, it would be illegal to. We aim to give you knowledge, not the piece of paper that allows you to legally practice with that knowledge. For that, you'd want to enter a matriculated program.
So I'll be like a lawyer without a law degree?
No no no. Completing our courses won't give you a medical or law degree, and they won't qualify you to take the boards or bar exams (the respective licensing bodies). It won't even get you a free night at a Holiday Inn Express.

You will simply be a better informed person who has taken our course.
So I can't use my newfound knowledge to save my neighbor's life?
Sure you could. Having medical/legal knowledge is valuable, but you cannot represent or imply that you are sanctioned by any licensing body as being qualified to give such advice.
